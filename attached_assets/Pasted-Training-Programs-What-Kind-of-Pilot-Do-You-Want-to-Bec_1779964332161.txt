Southern California market averages may range from $15,000–$25,000+ for a Private Pilot license or Instrument Rating.

Accelerated Flight School aims for approximately $7,500–$12,500 for a Private Pilot license depending on student consistency, weather, scheduling, study habits, and proficiency.

Purpose-Driven Maneuvers
Instead of simply telling a student, "today we are doing steep turns," we explain why the maneuver matters, how it improves aircraft control, how it is graded by the FAA, and how it directly connects to the checkride expectations.

Steep turns — A performance maneuver used to demonstrate precise aircraft control and coordination.

ACS — Airman Certification Standards, the official FAA grading checklist used for pilot tests.

Study Expectations
Students must study outside of flight lessons to progress efficiently. We are upfront about this from day one. Flight training is not just showing up and flying — students must study at home, understand the FAA materials, and prepare for the written test, oral exam, and flight portion. We help point students toward FAA study resources and books that are available free online so students know what to study and why it matters.

Proactive Scheduling

The Checkride Scheduling Advantage
The Hidden Cost

Many schools wait until all training is complete before trying to book a DPE checkride slot. In Southern California, this can create a 1 to 3-month delay.

That delay causes pilot rust, extra refresher flights, frustration, and added cost.

Our Approach

Accelerated Flight School coordinates checkride planning early once students demonstrate solid progress. The goal is to take the practical test shortly after training completion instead of waiting months and paying for extra training just to stay sharp.

Checkride — The FAA pilot test including an oral exam and a practical flight exam.

DPE — Designated Pilot Examiner, an FAA-authorized examiner who conducts checkrides.
